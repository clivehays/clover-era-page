The Evidence

Eight independent sources have named pieces of the same phenomenon.

AThe AI cognitive load shift

  1. Microsoft Research AI use shifts knowledge work from generation to evaluation. Heavy users report higher cognitive effort, not lower. 2025
  2. UC Berkeley Labor Center 67% of workers who adopted AI tools in 2025 reported working more hours, not fewer, by the end of the year. 2025
  3. Deloitte Mental fatigue and cognitive strain have surpassed workload volume as the leading predictors of burnout. 2025

BThe engagement and depletion phenomenon

  1. MetLife 56% of employees stay out of necessity, not commitment. Only 18% stay because they truly want to. 2026
  2. Gallup Manager engagement dropped 9 points since 2022. Global engagement at 20%, the lowest since 2020. 2026
  3. ActivTrak Behavioural data across 163,638 employees shows disengagement risk now exceeds burnout risk for the first time. 2026
  4. Eagle Hill Consulting 71% of middle managers report burnout, the highest of any job level. 2025
  5. McKinsey Quarterly The median S&P 500 company loses $228 million to $355 million annually to disengagement and attrition. 2023

The market has named this in pieces. We call the whole thing Silent Degradation.

Cost layer split

Across the cohort, 47% of total cost comes from voluntary departures. The other 53% comes from five layers most companies do not measure: disengagement, manager drag, promotion risk, innovation suppression, and customer impact.

The Measurement Method

How we measure what dashboards do not see.

Managers and team members answer the same six questions about the same workplace dimensions. The Manager Gap Index measures the gap between the two views. Across the cohort, the average gap is 70%. Most managers see less than a third of what their teams are actually experiencing.

V
Vulnerability

Can people be honest without risk? When something is wrong, do they say it or hide it?

When it drops: You get a team that says “everything's fine” in every meeting while building resentment underneath. The resignation comes out of nowhere because nobody felt safe flagging the problem.

Five other dimensions: Communication, Learning, Opportunity, Enablement, Reflection. Each one a measurable signal that maps to retention, performance, and growth. Each one with its own “when it drops” pattern.
