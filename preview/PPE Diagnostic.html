Where this number goes.

Current path With the lever addressed

Current path based on your projected trajectory. Improved path adds the productive capacity Bain's research suggests is recoverable when organisational drag is addressed.

What are you doing to influence this number sustainably, and how would you know if it was working?

Current path is your projected 24-month trajectory. Improved path adds the 20-25% productive capacity that Bain has documented is recoverable when organisational drag is addressed (Mankins and Garton, Time, Talent, Energy, 2017). This tool applies a conservative 15% recovery ceiling, moderated by your stated visibility into manager effectiveness. The five-year total extrapolates the year-2 run-rate forward at a constant level, with no further compounding past month 24.

Most executives cannot answer that question with data. They can answer it with instinct.

The phenomenon underneath this number has a name: silent degradation — the depletion of the conditions under which people perform, which does not appear on any dashboard. Across the eleven companies in the Q1 2026 cohort, the median annual loss runs to approximately $20 million.

The Manager Gap Index turns instinct into a quarterly trend line, six to twelve months ahead of where it shows up in the financials. It measures the one lever you have left.
