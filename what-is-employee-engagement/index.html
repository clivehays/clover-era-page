Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about employee engagement

What is the simple definition of employee engagement? +

Employee engagement is the emotional commitment an employee has to the organization and its goals. It means employees care about their work and company, going beyond just working for a paycheck.

What's the difference between employee engagement and satisfaction? +

Employee satisfaction means employees are content with their job and won't complain, but may not go the extra mile. Employee engagement means emotional commitment that drives discretionary effort and superior performance. Satisfied employees show up; engaged employees show up and deliver exceptional results.

How do you measure employee engagement? +

Employee engagement is measured through engagement surveys, pulse surveys, eNPS (employee Net Promoter Score), retention rates, productivity metrics, and observing discretionary effort behaviors. Key indicators include willingness to recommend the company, intent to stay, and pride in the organization.

What causes low employee engagement? +

Low engagement is caused by poor leadership, lack of recognition, limited growth opportunities, poor communication, work-life imbalance, inadequate resources, misalignment with company values, lack of autonomy, unfair treatment, and absence of meaningful work.

What are the key drivers of employee engagement? +

Key engagement drivers include meaningful work, career development opportunities, recognition and appreciation, trust in leadership, work-life balance, fair compensation, positive relationships with colleagues, alignment with organizational values, autonomy and empowerment, and clear communication.
